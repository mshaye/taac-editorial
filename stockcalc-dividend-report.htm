Company Description
Canadian Natural Resources is one of the largest oil and natural gas producers in western Canada, supplemented by operations in the North Sea and Offshore Africa. The company's portfolio includes light and medium oil, heavy oil, bitumen, synthetic oil, natural gas liquids, and natural gas. Production averaged 1.16 million barrels of oil equivalent per day in 2020, and the company estimates that it holds over 11.5 billion boe of proven and probable crude oil and natural gas reserves.

Dividend Glossary
Beta
This is a measure of the volatility, or systematic risk, of a security or a portfolio in comparison to the market as a whole.
Capital Expenditure
Funds used by a company to acquire or upgrade physical assets such as property, industrial buildings or equipment. Capital expenditures are generally depreciated or depleted over their useful life, as distinguished from repairs, which are subtracted from the income of the current year.
Current Debt
Represents the total amount of long-term debt such as bank loans and commercial paper, which is due within one year.
Declaration Date
The date the company announces it is paying a dividend.
Dividend
A payment to shareholders from the income earned by the company. Dividends can be paid to common or preferred shares
Dividend Coverage Ratio
This is calculated as the number of times a company could pay dividends to its common shareholders using its net income.
Dividend Growth Model
The expected annual dividend per share for the next year dividend by the required rate of return less the dividends projected growth rate.
Dividend Paydown Rate
This growth rate is the amount by which the initial share purchase price has been paid down by dividends received.
Dividend Growth Rate
This growth rate is calculated as the value of a dividend one year later divided by the current value of a dividend and is expressed as a percentage.
Dividend Yield
This is calculated as annual dividends per share (DPS) divided by stock price and is expressed as a percentage.
Earnings per Share (EPS)
This is the net income divided by the weighted average number of common shares outstanding.
Earnings Yield
This is calculated as earnings per share (EPS) divided by market price per share and is expressed as a percentage.
Ex-dividend date (Ex-date)
The date on which the dividend eligibility expires.
Financial Leverage
This is calculated as Total Assets divided by Common Equity. Note that: Common Equity = Total Shareholder’s Equity – Preferred Stock.
Interest Coverage
This is calculated as Earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) divided by the interest expense on the company’s debt.
Long Term Debt to Total Capital
This is calculated as Long Term Debt plus Capital Lease Obligations divided by Long Term Debt plus Capital Lease Obligation plus Total Shareholder’s Equity.
Payment Date
The date the dividend is paid to shareholders.
Payout Ratio
This is calculated as annual dividend per share (DPS) divided by earnings per share (EPS) and is expressed as a percentage. This is a measure of how much of a company’s earnings are paid in dividends.
Preferred Stock Dividends
The amount of dividends declared or paid in the period to preferred shareholders, or the amount for which the obligation to pay those dividends arose in the period.
Price to Earnings (PE) Ratio
This is calculated as the price of the stock dividend by the earnings per share for that stocks or as the reciprocal of the earnings yield (1 / Earnings Yield).
Record date
The cut-off date used to determine which shareholders are eligible to receive a dividend.
Return on Equity (ROE)
This is calculated as the net income divided by the company’s common equity as found on the balance sheet (generally average common equity between two periods is used).
